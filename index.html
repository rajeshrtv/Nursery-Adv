Plants

"A Beautiful Plant Is Like Having A Friend Around House"

Basil

Having antioxidants and anti-bacterial properties, basil helps maintain skin health, boosts digestion and detoxifies the body.

Amaryills

Their leaves and bulb contain an alkaloid, a chemical known to exhibit antitumoral, antiparasitic and other healing properties.

Hyacinth

Consume stir-fried hyacinth beans to treat digestive problems like diarrhea, flatulence, obesity and help soothe an inflamed throat.

Seeds

"From Small Seeds Grow Mighty Trees"

Flax

Flax seeds have been linked to health benefits, such as improved digestion and a reduced risk of heart disease and cancer.

Sesame

Sesame seeds are a good source of healthy fats, protein, B vitamins, minerals, fiber, antioxidants, and other beneficial plant compounds.

Pistachios

Pistachios can help keep your blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar. Pistachios are a nutritious addition to your diet.

Pots

"Wherever Life Plants You, Bloom With Grace"

Clay

Clay pots are readily available and good for growing plants. However, they can be expensive and heavy in large sizes, breakable if dropped, and many are subject to cracking in freezing temperatures.

Glazed Ceramic

These are the most colorful pots ranging from subtle earthen tones to playful. They are varied in style for plant growth. Most of them are subject to freeze damage, so they are a good investment.

Concrete

Attractive, durable, and affordable, concrete pots can be found for sale from local, wayside shops that manufacture them, as well as in highly styled forms from the trendiest shops.
